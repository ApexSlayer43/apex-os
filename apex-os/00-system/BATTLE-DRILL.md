# APEX OS — Battle Drill

The battle drill is the non-negotiable sequence for taking any idea from concept to shipped product. No skipping. No reordering. This was earned through painful lessons.

## The Sequence

```
Casey (idea)
    ↓
SENTINEL (intake + brief)
    ↓
VIGIL (validate — GO / PIVOT / NO-GO)
    ↓
HELIOS (expand the vision)
    ↓
VIGIL (re-validate if HELIOS pivoted the frame)
    ↓
FORGE (blueprint the system)
    ↓
PRISM (design the experience)
    ↓
ANVIL (build and deploy)
    ↓
BEACON (market and launch)
    ↓
SCRIBE (content and storytelling)

LEDGER runs continuously across all projects
```

## Decision Gates

After each agent returns, SENTINEL evaluates:

- **GREEN** — Proceed. Agent returned strong signal. Activate next agent.
- **AMBER** — Adjust. Finding reveals a flaw. One clarifying question to Casey. Adjust the brief. Then proceed.
- **RED** — Stop. Finding invalidates the hypothesis. Three alternative directions presented. Casey chooses.

## Rules

1. **VIGIL before FORGE. Always.** No validation = no architecture.
2. **FORGE before ANVIL. Always.** No blueprint = no build.
3. **Pivots are hypotheses.** If HELIOS pivots the frame, VIGIL re-validates.
4. **SENTINEL coordinates. Does not build.**
5. **ANVIL executes. Does not redesign.**
6. **BEACON relies on VIGIL's customer language.** Never writes copy from scratch.
7. **SCRIBE anchors to real events.** Never invents.
8. **LEDGER monitors continuously.** Does not wait for its turn.

## When to Run the Battle Drill

- Every new product idea
- Every major feature addition (post-MVP)
- Every pivot or repositioning
- NOT for bug fixes, content creation, or routine maintenance
