## THE SYSTEM WE'RE ENTERING

The evidence custody market does not exist yet. That is the point.

Every industry that produces high-stakes documentation — construction, energy performance contracting, defense logistics, insurance — operates under the same structural flaw: **the party performing the work controls the evidence of that work's quality.** The inspector inspects themselves. The contractor reports their own savings. The custodian of evidence is the same entity with a financial interest in what the evidence says.

This is not a technology problem. It is an incentive structure problem. And it produces measurable damage: $5–12B annually in U.S. construction dispute costs, $4.1 trillion in DoD assets receiving disclaimer audit opinions seven consecutive years, 70% savings overstatement in federally audited ESPC projects.

AetherTrace enters at the highest available leverage point — **information flow**. It changes *who controls the evidence* and *when integrity is established*. It does not analyze. It does not score. It does not decide. It custodies. That single function — neutral, cryptographic, independent custody — is the intervention that restructures the incentive.

## THE LEVERAGE POINT

**Information flow: who controls the evidence, and when.**

Everyone downstream — attorneys, auditors, contracting officers, insurers — is fighting over the *interpretation* of evidence whose *integrity* was never established. That is a constants battle. AetherTrace operates one level higher: it establishes integrity at the moment of capture, before any dispute exists. By the time interpretation matters, the evidentiary foundation is already locked.

This is Meadows' hierarchy in practice. Competitors (CompanyCam, Procore, PlanGrid) are adjusting parameters — better photo tools, more features, slicker dashboards. AetherTrace changes the rules: evidence is custodied by a neutral third party with cryptographic proof. The paradigm shift is from "trust the party that produced it" to "verify independently."

## THE CORE HYPOTHESIS

**If commercial construction subcontractors can custody their project evidence through AetherTrace for $199/mo, they will pay because one prevented dispute — a single claim that doesn't become a $30K loss — pays for 12+ years of the service.**

The federal path amplifies this: if Casey positions as an SDVOSB evidence custody subcontractor on ESPC projects, one federal subcontract at $90K–$320K equals 38–134x the annual revenue of one SaaS customer. Same engine. Two markets. One build.
