01 —— The Mark — Two Candidates
OPTION A
The Link
Chain · Continuity · Custody

A single chain link viewed from above. Angular corners, not circular — precision machined, not playful. The oval negative space is the opening where the next link connects. Reads immediately at any scale. The shape IS the function.

OPTION B
The Seal
Authority · Permanence · Certification

A hexagonal signet seal — the oldest form of authenticated custody in human history, modernized with precision geometry. The inner chain mark says: evidence lives inside. The hexagon says: this has been sealed by authority that predates you.

05 —— Brand Voice — The Brand Sounds Like a Fact

The founding line

"You did the work. You took the photos. You still lost the claim."

On the technology

"This isn't a promise. It's math."

On neutrality

"AetherTrace cannot modify evidence after ingestion."

On the GC problem

"Your evidence. Your custody. The GC can't revoke what they never controlled."

On pricing

"One $30K claim. 12.5 years covered."

The brand always —

Declarative sentences. No questions in positioning copy.

Numbers over adjectives. $30K not "significant claims."

Pain before solution. The sub's experience first, always.

The brand never raises its voice. Math doesn't yell.

The brand never —

Uses "seamless," "robust," "powerful," or "leverage."

Says "get started." Says "Protect your work."

Calls evidence "files" or "documents." Says "sealed."

Competes with other software. Competes with phone photos.

06 —— The Decision Frame

Option A — The Link

Reads as: continuity, chain, the moment of connection. The mark says "I am part of something that cannot be broken." Stronger for the product UI — shows up in the chain visualization naturally. The link is the evidence. The mark IS what you're building.

Option B — The Seal

Reads as: authority, permanence, institutional weight. The mark says "this was certified here. It doesn't move." Stronger for the federal and legal audiences — DoD, attorneys, arbitrators. The hexagon is ancient and modern simultaneously.

HELIOS — The question to carry

Option A says what you build — the chain. Option B says what you are — the authority. The sub who just got burned doesn't need authority. They need something that holds. Casey — which one do you show a contractor at 6am before they head to the jobsite?
