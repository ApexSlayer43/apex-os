# VIGIL Intelligence Report — SDVOSB Federal Energy Subcontractor Re-Validation

## Mission
Re-validate AetherTrace go-to-market in light of Casey's strategic reframe: instead of only selling SaaS to subcontractors (Path A), also position as an SDVOSB-certified evidence custody subcontractor on federal energy projects (Path B). Assess whether Casey's military background + SDVOSB certification + AetherTrace creates a viable federal contracting position.

## Verdict
✅ GO — Confidence: HIGH (Path B validated as parallel track, not replacement)

## Load-Bearing Assumption
Federal prime contractors on large energy/construction projects (ESPCs) have mandatory small business subcontracting requirements that create real, funded demand for SDVOSB subcontractors — and "evidence custody" can be positioned as a legitimate, needed service within those projects.

**Status: VERIFIED — with nuance.**

The demand side is structural and confirmed. The 5% SDVOSB goal (raised from 3% by NDAA FY2024) applies to both prime and subcontract dollars. The annual federal target now exceeds $31 billion. Every large prime contractor on a federal construction or energy contract exceeding $2 million must submit a small business subcontracting plan with specific SDVOSB goals. This is not optional — it's FAR 19.7.

The nuance: "evidence custody" is not an existing federal service category. Casey would be creating the category, not filling an existing one. This is both the risk and the moat.

## Signal Map

### Pain Signal: STRONG

**ESPC Savings Overstatement — The Structural Problem**
- GAO found that contractors' reports overstated cost and energy savings for 14 of 20 ESPC projects reviewed
- One project had over $104,000 in annual savings reported but not achieved — 40% of the project's total
- The party performing the energy work controls the evidence of savings — identical structural flaw to construction
- DoD clean audit deadline: December 31, 2028. DoD IG found the goal is "in jeopardy." Congressional action imminent if failed.
- DoD spending $1B/year on audit remediation and still receiving disclaimer opinions 7 consecutive years
- New material weakness identified: DoD cannot track government property in the possession of contractors. Auditors need evidence the contractors can't or won't provide.

**Behavioral Evidence — SDVOSB ESCOs Already Exist**
- HSGS Energy operates as an SDVOSB subcontractor to Ameresco (one of the largest ESCOs) on federal ESPC projects. This is direct behavioral evidence that SDVOSB subs participate in ESPC work.
- The VA created a dedicated SDVOSB ESPC IDIQ in May 2020, awarding to four SDVOSB companies, with two more on-ramped in March 2022. The VA is actively seeking SDVOSB capacity for energy projects.
- A VA ESPC task order awarded to an SDVOSB had a project investment value of nearly $3.0 million.

**Quote from GAO (GAO-15-432):** Agencies lacked adequate processes for reviewing ESCO M&V reports, and savings may be overstated because the party guaranteeing the savings is the same party measuring and reporting them.

This is the same structural flaw AetherTrace was designed to address: the party performing the work controls the evidence of quality.

### Market Signal: STRONG

- **SDVOSB federal goal: 5% of all prime and subcontract dollars** — raised from 3% by NDAA FY2024. Annual target exceeds $31 billion across the federal government.
- **SDVOSB sole-source threshold: $5 million** (services) / $8.5 million (manufacturing). A contracting officer can award directly to Casey without competition up to $5M if no reasonable expectation of receiving two or more SDVOSB offers.
- **SBA VetCert processing: 12 days average** (down from 81 days in late 2024). The certification pipeline is clear and functional.
- **ESPC project size range: $200K to $18M per task order** (ENABLE data). Even a 3-5% subcontract slice of a mid-range $5M project = $150K-$250K per task order.
- **ESPC total investment: $5.7 billion in federal facility energy efficiency** (FY2005-2017). The pipeline is large and ongoing.
- **FAR 19.7 mandate:** Every federal construction contract >$2M with subcontracting possibilities requires a small business subcontracting plan with SDVOSB goals. Primes need SDVOSB subs to meet these goals. Casey becomes the solution to their compliance requirement.

## Risks

1. **Past performance gap** — Federal contracting heavily weights past performance. Casey has none yet. Mitigation: Path A builds the track record. Also, SDVOSB sole-source awards have more flexibility — contracting officers can weight SDVOSB status over past performance when the need is clear.
   Signal to watch: first 3-5 SaaS customers and whether their use cases generate documentation strong enough for a capability statement.

2. **Category creation risk** — "Evidence custody" is not an existing line item in ESPC task orders. Casey must convince primes that this is a service they need to subcontract. Mitigation: GAO's own reports make the case. Frame it as "independent M&V evidence integrity" — a service that protects the prime from audit findings.
   Signal to watch: first conversation with an ESCO — do they recognize the problem immediately, or does it require extensive education?

3. **Federal contracting overhead** — FAR compliance, DCAA accounting, insurance requirements. These are real costs for a solo founder. Mitigation: Start as subcontractor (not prime). The prime handles most compliance burden. Casey's SDVOSB status is the asset; the prime handles the paperwork.
   Signal to watch: research DCAA-compliant accounting requirements before Month 4. If the overhead is >$5K/year, it's still worth it on one $90K subcontract.

4. **ESPC program instability** — ENABLE was retired in March 2025. Gen 3 IDIQ ordering period expires April 2026. Gen 4 is active but the landscape is shifting. Mitigation: Evidence custody is not ESPC-specific. DoD construction, MILCON, facility maintenance — all have the same structural flaw.
   Signal to watch: Gen 4 IDIQ awards and whether new ESCOs are added or dropped.

## ACH Results — Hypothesis Elimination

**H1: SDVOSB set-asides create real demand for evidence custody as a subcontracted service.**
Evidence consistent: 5% SDVOSB goal is statutory. FAR 19.7 mandates subcontracting plans. VA created SDVOSB ESPC IDIQ. HSGS operates as SDVOSB ESPC sub. GAO documents the savings overstatement problem that evidence custody solves.
Evidence inconsistent: None found.
**H1 survives.**

**H2: SDVOSB demand exists but evidence custody isn't a recognized service — primes won't carve out a sub slot for it.**
Evidence consistent: No existing SDVOSB offers evidence custody. It's not a standard ESPC line item.
Evidence inconsistent: Primes need SDVOSB dollars to meet goals — they actively seek SDVOSB subs. The service doesn't need to be standard; it needs to be useful and countable. GAO's own findings create the demand narrative. The ESCO's M&V self-reporting is a documented audit risk.
**H2 weakened but not eliminated.** This is the hypothesis to test in customer conversations. The first ESCO conversation will resolve it.

**H3: SDVOSB requirements exist on paper but relationships are locked — new entrants can't break in.**
Evidence consistent: Federal contracting does favor established relationships.
Evidence inconsistent: SDVOSB sole-source threshold is $5M — a contracting officer can bypass competition entirely. VetCert backlog cleared, signaling SBA is actively encouraging new SDVOSBs to enter. VA on-ramped two new SDVOSBs to their ESPC IDIQ in 2022 — the door is not shut.
**H3 weakened significantly.** Sole-source authority is the override mechanism.

## Verdict Logic

The SDVOSB federal subcontractor angle is validated as a parallel go-to-market path. The evidence is structural: the 5% SDVOSB goal is statutory, the FAR mandates subcontracting plans on large contracts, ESPC projects have a GAO-documented evidence integrity problem, and no SDVOSB currently offers cryptographic evidence custody. Casey's competitive stack — veteran status + SDVOSB certification + AetherTrace technology + military operations background — is unique and non-replicable.

This does not replace the SaaS play. It amplifies it. Path A builds the technology credibility. Path B captures the high-value federal contracts. Both paths use the same engine. The architecture must remain domain-agnostic (HELIOS's directive holds).

What would flip this verdict: Evidence that ESCOs have no flexibility in subcontracting (contradicted by HSGS/Ameresco relationship). Evidence that SDVOSB sole-source authority is being restricted (contradicted by FAR revisions expanding it). Evidence that GAO's savings overstatement findings are being addressed through other means (no evidence of this — the problem persists).
